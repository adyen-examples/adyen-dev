Working with Webhooks
Webhooks are an essential feature of the Adyen platform, delivering critical events such as payment outcomes, new chargebacks, report availability, and updates on merchant onboarding. But unlike REST APIs, the asynchronous nature of webhooks introduces unique challenges for developers especially in terms of testing and troubleshooting.

To support developers, we have created a range of resources, including a Getting Started video, a blog with Best Practices for consuming webhooks, and a newly released Developer Guide for testing webhooks in the local development environment. Our integration-examples are also a valuable resource, offering code snippets in various languages to help you receive, validate and process webhook events.

Transfer API v4
The Transfer API and Webhooks Version 4 represent a step toward simplifying a platform's integration effort. New fields provide more context, such as 'categoryData' with relevant information about bank transfers, platform payments, and Adyen-issued cards. Additionally, the new Transaction Webhook is available to inform you about incoming and outgoing transfers in your platform.

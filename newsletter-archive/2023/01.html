January Developer Newsletter
Hi folks,

On behalf of the Developer Relations Team: The best wishes for 2023!

This year, our team is on a mission. A mission where we try to maximize product value for our developers so they can integrate easier and faster by acting as a Tech focused bridge between Adyen and its merchants through Community, Coding and Content. Expect new integration-examples, tools to show up on our Github, as well as new technical content and best practices that are relevant to you.

One thing that we aim at improving this year, is getting more feedback from our readers. This brings us to our main topic for this newsletter: If there's one thing we could do for you today, what would that be and how can we help? Let us know on Twitter or reach out to us on devrel@adyen.com.

With that being said, here's our first Developer’s Newsletter for 2023: blogs, conferences, meetups and our new releases of libraries and plugins!
Upcoming events
January 31 - WordPress Meetup, Amsterdam
On Tuesday 31st of January 19:00, we will host our WordPress Meetup at the Adyen HQ in Amsterdam. There will be two speakers: Anne-Mieke Bovelett, an accessibility advocate, will talk about what impact website accessibility on your search engine optimalization and its conversion. Ramon Fincken, a hosting entrepreneur, will then follow up with his talk on how to become a more sustainable WordPress developer. Free drinks and pizza included!

February 1 - Adyen & Elastic Meetup, Amsterdam
On Tuesday 1st of February 17:30, we will host the Elastic Meetup at the Adyen HQ in Amsterdam. Elasticsearch is a well known tool for enabling search. We will have two speakers: Eavan Pattie, a Java Developer from the Observability team at Adyen will walk you through a handful of adventures they had to go through in their Elasticsearch cluster. In the second talk, Sander Philipse, a senior software engineer at Elastic, will walk you through how you can easily ingest search data from a number of sources using their beta connector framework. As always, food, drinks and awesome people included! Will you join us too?

February 16 - Adyen Tech Software Testing Meetup, Amsterdam
On Thursday 16th of February 17:30, we will host our Adyen Tech Software testing Meetup at the Adyen HQ in Amsterdam. There will be two speakers: Peter Schuler, from the Rabobank, will give a talk about how the general approach to unit testing might harm your flexibility and how a few simple changes in your approach will make your life better. We'll follow up with a talk by Tim van der Lippe, Staff Engineer at Adyen and a core maintainer of Mockito. He'll share his vision on how to effectively use mocks in tests. We will have food and drinks during the event, so join us!

Latest Releases
Adyen Web 5.31.1 
  • Restrict Blik input field to digits only.
  • We now stop calling `onError(..)` during 3DS2 fingerprinting.
  • Added buttonRootNode property to GooglePay.
  • Added config option disableIOSArrowKeys.
  • We now validate postal code when billingAddressMode is partial.
Adyen Android 4.9.1 
  • For cards, when the shopper enters a card number and a dual branded card is detected, a brand is no longer selected by default.
  • Drop-in no longer crashes when navigating back after the shopper removes all stored payment methods.
Adyen iOS 4.10.3 
  • Fixed an issue where Apple Pay in Drop-in would not close.
  • Apple Pay now provides the complete list of networks supported by shopper's iOS device.
Adyen Magento2 8.13.0 
  • Allow merchants (with headless integrations) to specify recurring processing model in the request.
  • Stored Klarna Pay Later as recurring in Magento vault.
  • Fixed Adyen Pay by Link for virtual products.
  • Fixed Adyen Giving for iDeal payments.
  • Added configuration to Magento admin to setup the live domain file for ApplePay.
  • Added support for GooglePay donations.
Adyen Salesforce Commerce Cloud 22.2.2 
  • Fixed sending multiple emails when confirmation page is refreshed (SiteGenesis).
  • Allow users to use multiple giftcard brands instead of just one.
  • Removed custom 3DS settings in the configuration page.
